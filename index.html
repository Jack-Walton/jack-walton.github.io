Opus Electus

Music Information Retrieval in Bandcamp
A graduate paper for the University of Missouri. The paper is an APA-style, user experience (UX) literature review. At the time, I was enrolled as a library and information science student. The paper received full marks from three peer reviewers and the professor. In this paper, I analyze the Bandcamp information system against peer-reviewed literature to derive auspicious web design ideas.
HIM Configuration Help
A brief instruction manual that I wrote for Oracle Health. The manual is for a cloud-based application that integrates the Optum web encoder with the Cerner Health Information Management (HIM) solution. This technology helps streamline the highly-regulated financial transactions between clinics and insurance companies. It also forms the backbone of an accurate electronic health record. Subject-matter expertise was provided by the Cerner HIM product and engineering teams. Editorial guidance was prodived by my documentation architect. It is hosted on the Cerner Confluence instance, CernerWiki.
Saint Didier's Flowering Verge...
A peer-reviewed article I wrote in collaboration with my research mentor during the 2020 Lockdown. The article was published in the art history journal, Different Visions, which is supported by St. Olaf College (where Kierkegaard's papers are archived) and Oklahoma State University. In this paper, I document an illuminated manuscript held at the Nelson-Atkins Museum of Art. The manuscript is analyzed both qualitatively and quantitatively, using traditional academic methods, as well as digital humanities approaches. I also briefly wax philosophical about the impact of religion, art, and language on the march of history through apocalyptic, world-historical events.
