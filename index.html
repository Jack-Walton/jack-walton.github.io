Welcome

You have found Jack Spencer Walton's Web Portfolio. Jack is a technical writer currently based in Kansas City. Over the last two years, Jack has authored documentation for several Oracle Health (formerly known as Cerner) Revenue Cycle solutions. Interests include information architecture, historical linguistics, web design, and electronic music. In spare time, Jack likes to pick up shifts at the Kansas City Public Library, where they often work at the reference and archives desks. Jack graduated magna cum laude from the University of Missouri - Kansas City with a Bachelor of Arts in English Language and Literature. As an undergraduate, they worked as a research assistant for CODICES Digital Humanities Lab. For more information, please see the resume below. Thank you.

Selected Work

The items below represent a sample of written work. Many of these articles are peer-reviewed. Thus, Jack cannot claim responsibility for every editorial and design decision made in these documents. They do, however, represent interests and the scope of abilities. The documents in which Jack is the sole author are presented first.

Music Information Retrieval in Bandcamp
This document is a short paper written while Jack was a graduate student at the University of Missouri - Columbia. At the time, they were enrolled as a Library and Information Science student. Jack ultimately decided to withdraw from the program to focus on technical writing, but the experience sparked an interest in information architecture. In this paper, the music information retrieval system of Bandcamp.com is analyzed against peer-reviewed literature to develop information architecture guidelines.
Summer Undergraduate Research Opportunity (SUROP) Grant Proposal
While Jack was an undergraduate research assistant, they applied for a grant to fund a research expedition to the Getty in Los Angeles. The mission was to research medieval manuscripts to find French and Latin narrative exemplars for a manuscript held at the Nelson Atkins Museum of Art. Due to the COVID-19 lockdown in the Spring of 2020, the grant was not funded. However, Jack was awarded a certificate of achievement for the quality of the document.
Health Benefit Management Help Pages
This wiki space is a public-facing set of help documentation written for the Department of Defense. The content is intended to help administrators at veteran's hospitals navigate complex benefits plans.
HIM Configuration Help
This wiki space is for use with a cloud-based application to integrate the Optum Cloud Encoder with the Cerner Health Information Management system. This technology helps streamline the highly-regulated financial transactions between clinics and payers.
A Quantitative Analysis of the Royer Didier
This blog post was written while Jack was an undergraduate researcher for CODICES digital humanities lab.
Saint Didier's Flowering Verge...
This peer-reviewed article was written with a research mentor. It uses chemical, computational, and traditional literary and art historical analysis to provide a complete set of documentation for an illuminated manuscript held at the Nelson Atkins Museum of Art. This project required translating documents from French, Latin, and Italian to English.
